Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy source. A common example of phishing is when a user receives a fake email or message that appears to be from a bank or a popular website.

In a typical phishing scenario, a student might receive an email claiming that their bank account needs urgent verification. The email contains a link that looks genuine but actually leads to a fake website. When the user enters their login details or OTP, the attacker captures this information and gains access to their account.

Students and young users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences of phishing can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting online and verifying the authenticity of messages before taking any action.
